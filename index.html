Our First Weekend Trip to New York City!

In May of 2015, our family made it's first visit to the Big Apple.
Below are some highlights of our trip, including pictures and links (should you want to learn more.)

THE SITES
  • Ed Sullivan Theater

    • We arrived in NYC on May 21, 2015 ... the day after David Letterman's final Late Show aired.
    When we passed the famous theater, they still had the David Letterman signage up, so we caught a piece of history!
    You can even see the famous Angelo's Pizza, used by Letterman to sneak out onto the streets of Manhattan after his final taping.

  • Trinity Church & Cemetery

    • Trinity Church has been significant to New York City's history for over 300 years. It was first chartered by King William III in 1697 as part of the Church of England, but later became an Episcopal church, after the American Revolution.
    Both George Washington and Alexander Hamilton worshiped there.

THE MUSEUMS
  • Metropolitan Museum of Art

    • The Met presents over 5,000 years of art from around the world for everyone to experience and enjoy.
    We were disappointed that we only had a couple of hours to spend in this iconic museum, but were glad for what few works of art we were able to view.

  • The Whitney Museum

    • The Whitneyâ€™s collection contains some of the most significant and exciting work created by artists in the United States during the twentieth and twenty-first centuries.

  • American Museum of Natural History

    • The American Museum of Natural History is one of the largest museums in the world. It's located across the street from Central Park and is made up of 28 interconnected buildings, plus a planetarium and a library. This museum's collections contain over 33 million artifacts.
    Needless to say, we only saw a fraction of them on our short, weekend visit.
    The animal dioramas and the Dinosaurs were what we focused on this trip.

THE FOOD
  • Bar Americain

    • Our favorite chef to watch on Food Network is Bobby Flay, so the first place we targeted for dinner was one of his restaurants.
    Bar Americain was right on our way, walking from our hotel in mid-town to the play we were going to see in the Theater District.
    (We actually planned the timing of our trip so we could see Jim Parsons (who stars as Sheldon in "Big Bang Theory")
    in the off-Broadway production, "An Act of God".)
    Neither the restaurant nor the play were a disappointment!

  • Eataly

    • Eataly is the love child of Iron Chef Mario Batali.
    He created a vibrant marketplace for all things related to Italian food of only the finest quality.
    You can shop here, take classes, or sit at one of the many islands where chefs will cook your food right in front of you.
    Glad we didn't skip this one!
